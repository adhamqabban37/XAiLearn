Lesson 1.2: A Brief History of Artificial Intelligence

This lesson explores the fascinating origins of AI, from early concepts in philosophy and fiction to the landmark moments that defined the field. We'll journey through the key milestones, from the Turing Test to the AI winters, and celebrate the pioneers who paved the way for the technology we see today.

Key Points:

  • The Turing Test (1950): Alan Turing proposes a test of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.
  • The Dartmouth Workshop (1956): The term "Artificial Intelligence" is coined, and the field is formally born as a research discipline.
  • The "AI Winters": Periods of reduced funding and interest in AI research, often following periods of excessive hype.
  • Expert Systems (1980s): The rise of AI programs that mimic the decision-making ability of a human expert in a narrow domain.
  • Deep Blue vs. Garry Kasparov (1997): A major milestone where a computer defeated a reigning world chess champion for the first time.
